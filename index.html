The Science

Chemistry and electrochemistry behind clean transport technologies.

🔥 Combustion of Petrol (Octane)

2C₈H₁₈ + 25O₂ → 16CO₂ + 18H₂O + Energy

Each litre produces ~2.31 kg CO₂. At 15 km/L = ~154 g CO₂/km.

Show energy analysis ↓

Octane has 34.7 MJ/L energy density. ICE engines waste 70–80% as heat, converting only 20–30% into kinetic energy. This thermodynamic limitation makes EVs fundamentally more efficient.

🌿 Ethanol Fermentation

C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂

CO₂ released ≈ CO₂ absorbed during photosynthesis — near-carbon-neutral cycle.

Show carbon cycle ↓

Sugarcane absorbs ~12 tonnes CO₂/hectare/year. Burning this ethanol releases the same CO₂ back — closing the cycle. Net lifecycle reduction: 60–75% vs. petrol.

💧 Hydrogen Fuel Cell

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O + Electrical Energy

Only byproduct: water. Fuel cell efficiency 50–60% vs. ICE 20–30%.

Show efficiency comparison ↓

Well-to-wheel: Diesel 20% → Hydrogen FC 30–35% → Battery EV 70–80%. But hydrogen wins for long-haul: 10-min refueling vs. hours of charging, 500+ km range at 40-tonne payload.
